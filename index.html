About me

I’m Chris House, a front-end web developer from Kansas City. I’ve been creating websites personally since 2009 and professionally since 2011. In that time I’ve learned how to listen to clients, to understand precisely what they’re needing and to surpass their expectations. My goal as a web developer is to make the client’s life easy by bridging the gap between their vision and a real functioning website. Nothing excites me more than delivering a product that makes both the client and the end user happy.


In addition to my expertise in HTML, CSS, JavaScript and WordPress, I’m addicted to learning new languages, tools and frameworks. My latest forays have included React.js, Jekyll, and CSS Grid Layout. I learn best by listening to others, so I’d be lost without all the brilliant minds whose blogs, books, conference talks and podcasts I digest regularly.


When I’m not immersing myself in all things web, you’ll find me just enjoying life with my wife Samantha and my sons Vincent and Victor. I love reading horror and sci-fi novels, playing my guitar and discovering great movies.
